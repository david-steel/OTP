The Intelligent Organization

A founding manifesto for Organizational Intelligence Management.

The companies that define the next decade will not simply use AI. They will manage AI as part of the workforce, giving digital agents roles, goals, memory, KPIs, managers, and real accountability. This book is the case for that shift, and the playbook for making it.

For decades, intelligence stayed trapped inside people, meetings, documents, and tools. CRM organized customers. ERP organized resources. HRIS organized employees. OTP organizes organizational intelligence, the decisions, lessons, commitments, and digital labor that every company depends on and almost every company forgets.

Across twelve chapters, The Intelligent Organization names the cost of forgetting, reframes AI as digital labor, and lays out the frameworks that hold every contributor, human or digital, accountable to the same work. Read it in order or jump to any chapter below.
