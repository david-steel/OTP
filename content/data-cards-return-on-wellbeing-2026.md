# Data Cards — Return on Wellbeing 2026 (OTP angle)

**Source for all figures:** Wellhub, *Return on Wellbeing 2026 — Building a Top Talent Moat in the Age of AI.* Survey of 1,515 HR leaders across 10 markets, fielded January 6–26, 2026. Some employee-side figures are from Wellhub's companion *State of Work-Life Wellness 2026* (noted as WLW).

Five standalone cards. Each is sized for a LinkedIn image, an X post, or a slide. Stat on top, OTP point underneath. No em dashes, OTP voice. Use one per day or as a carousel.

---

## Card 1 — The adoption gap

**88% of companies already use AI. Only 7% have actually scaled it.**

Almost everyone has started. Almost no one has finished. The knowing is universal, the doing is rare. That space between "we use AI" and "AI is part of how we run" is the whole game, and you do not cross it with another tool. You cross it by giving the work a seat, an owner, and a number.

*Wellhub, Return on Wellbeing 2026*

---

## Card 2 — The reality gap

**77% of executives think employee wellbeing improved last year. Only 33% of employees agree.**

A 44-point gap between what leadership believes and what the team actually experiences. The report's line: "you cannot close a gap you do not know is there." This is what running on intuition costs you. Nothing is forcing reality to surface, so the gap stays invisible until it walks out the door.

*Wellhub, Return on Wellbeing 2026*

---

## Card 3 — Shadow AI

**78% of AI users bring their own tools to work. 52% hide that they used AI.**

More than half of the AI work in your company is happening where you cannot see it, done by your most resourceful people, who would rather you did not know. That is not a security footnote. It is the early shape of a two-tier workforce, split by who figured it out first. The fix is to bring the agents onto the chart, not push them further into the dark.

*Wellhub, Return on Wellbeing 2026*

---

## Card 4 — Ops became a CFO problem

**96% of companies say Finance now significantly influences workforce-planning decisions.**

The way your company coordinates work is no longer an HR topic or an ops topic. It is a financial one. When the people deciding headcount and budget are looking at how the business actually runs, "we have a system that makes execution happen every week" stops being a nice-to-have and starts being a number on someone's spreadsheet.

*Wellhub, Return on Wellbeing 2026*

---

## Card 5 — Wellbeing is the new salary

**86% of employees say their wellbeing at work matters as much as their pay. 85% would leave a company that ignores it.**

Salary stopped being the only thing that holds your best people. How it feels to work inside your operating cadence (the overload, the clarity, the sense that someone sees the real picture) is now a retention number. The execution system you build is not just an efficiency play. It is a talent moat.

*Wellhub, Return on Wellbeing 2026 (employee figures: State of Work-Life Wellness 2026)*

## Bonus stats in reserve (for posts, decks, or replies)

- AI-skilled workers earned **56% more** than peers without those skills in 2024, up from a 25% gap the year before. *(PwC, cited in ROW 2026)*
- **62%** of HR leaders are worried about losing employees with in-demand AI skills.
- **75%** of HR leaders say managers are overwhelmed by expanding responsibilities; **51%** call manager overload a significant risk to company performance. *(Gartner, cited in ROW 2026)*
- **88%** of organizations say retaining top performers is a top priority for 2026.
- Of companies that measure wellness ROI, **95% report a positive return.**
- Only **39%** of organizations feel prepared to support their people through change and disruption.
