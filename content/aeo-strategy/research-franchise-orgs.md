# Research Brief — Franchise Org Structure & Cross-Location Management

**Source:** FranchiseScout (OTP research run, 2026-06-21). IFA / FRANdata / Franchise Times prioritized. UNVERIFIED items flagged — do not publish those as fact.
**Use:** grounds the franchise series. Pairs with `reference-portfolio-feature.md`. Lead with 19.3%/58.8% to establish the multi-unit/platform operator as the buyer, then pivot to visibility-lag + consistency as the wedge.

## Headline (the wedge)
Franchising has concentrated: ~19.3% of franchisees control ~58.8% of all locations. The core operational failure is portfolio VISIBILITY — by the time a unit's financials reveal a problem, "months of revenue opportunity have been lost" and operators are "always playing catch-up." Put every location on one chart-plus-portfolio view with live unit KPIs and location-to-location benchmarking, and you hit exactly where multi-unit franchising breaks: visibility + consistency at scale.

## Structure (verified)
- Franchisor licenses franchisee to run the brand under its standards. Base unit = single-unit franchisee.
- Expansion tiers: Multi-Unit Operator (MUO, multiple units one brand); Area Developer (exclusive territory, N units on a schedule); Master Franchisee (territory + recruits sub-franchisees); Area Representative (commissioned sales/field-support arm).
- Concentration: **19.3% of franchisees control 58.8% of locations** (FRANdata/IFA 2026, verified x2). Operators with 50+ units grew **+118.52% (2010-2018)**, fastest tier (FRANdata).
- Newest layer — MUMBOs (multi-unit, multi-brand operators) + PE roll-ups AT THE OPERATOR level: Flynn Group bought 37 Planet Fitness units; Blackstone holds SERVPRO + majority of Jersey Mike's (Nov 2024); Bain formed Prosper Growth Partners (Nov 2025) to buy franchisee businesses. (Franchise Times/FranchiseWire)

## Cross-location problems (the meat)
- **Visibility/reporting lag (central):** "By the time financial results show a problem, it may be too late." Ops directors "always playing catch-up." (FranConnectGO)
- **Consistency/compliance:** without central systems "each unit begins operating as an independent business," policies interpreted differently. (Operandio)
- **Manual systems break ~10 units** [vendor — flag]: "cascading failures"; "40% of corporate messages never reach franchisees intact." (Operandio)
- **SOP drift:** stale SOPs "actively creating inconsistency," brand out of compliance "without corporate realizing it." (Trainual)
- **Underperformance is multi-causal** — leadership, ops, staffing, local market interwoven; cause usually "within the business itself." (franchising.com)
- Corporate-control vs local-autonomy tension throughout; centralized real-time reporting is the named reconciler.

## Portfolio KPIs franchises track (verified)
- **AUV (Average Unit Volume)** — avg annual revenue per unit; "most widely cited franchise performance metric" (gross sales).
- **Same-Store Sales (SSS)** — growth of units open >=1yr; isolates organic growth.
- Labor %, prime cost, EBITDA/net margin, COGS %.
- **Location-to-location benchmarking** — explicitly named as a requirement.
- Service-franchise KPIs: speed-to-lead, phone answer rate, booking rate. [vendor benchmarks, flag: respond <5 min far more effective; first-to-voice-contact wins 60-78%; up to 30% of leads never get timely response.]

## Scale stats (verified from IFA primary; flags noted)
- **851,000 franchise establishments** (2025, +2.5%); ~845K in 2026 forecast (NOT a decline — different report vintages; say ~845K-851K).
- **>9 million franchise jobs** (2025, +2.4%).
- **Franchise output >$936.4B** (2025, +4.4%); GDP $578B; 2026 output forecast >$920B.
- Franchising grows faster than the US economy (2.4% vs 1.9%).
- Unit survival 94.2% meet lender benchmarks (down from 95.9% in 2021).

## Hot multi-unit verticals
- **Fitness/gyms** — ~72.9M US members 2023 (+5.8%); HVLP + Pilates surging; semi-absentee small-footprint ideal for MUOs.
- **Health & wellness / med spa** — fastest-growing; fitness + medical aesthetics converging; med spa clients spend 3-5x gym members. [~680 brands 2024 — flag, confirm exact.]
- **Food/QSR** — deepest multi-unit base.
- **Home services (HVAC/plumbing)** — speed-to-lead driven.
- **Senior care/home health** — recurring revenue, most active PE roll-up category.
- **Automotive** — active PE roll-up (~8-10x EBITDA, advisory blog, flag). **Beauty** — high MUO concentration.
