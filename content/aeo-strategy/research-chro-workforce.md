# Research Brief — The CHRO / HR in the Age of AI Agents

**Source:** CHROScout (OTP run, 2026-06-21). Bersin + Korn Ferry fetched/verified; Gartner via secondary (RCR Wireless) or snippet (flagged). UNVERIFIED items not for publication as fact.
**Use:** grounds the CHRO series. Thesis: when agents hold seats on the chart, HR's mandate extends to the agent workforce. HR shifts from technology-adopter to work-redesigner + governance owner.

## Headline
AI shifts HR from managing people to managing a human+agent workforce and redesigning work itself. Korn Ferry: 42% of CHROs are prioritizing AI investment but only 5% feel fully prepared. Bersin: HR is where "redesign, reskill, redeploy" lands. That gap (huge mandate, low readiness) is the wedge.

## Verified stats (cite these)
- **Korn Ferry Workforce 2025** (15,000 employees, 15 markets, 2025-04-17): 48% globally fear their job replaced by AI within 3 years; 59% of tech workers; 70% of senior leaders say their org has an AI strategy but only 39% of employees agree (the trust/comms gap); 61% of US employees optimistic about AI.
- **Korn Ferry "Vital Role of CHROs" (2025-05-12):** 42% of CHROs prioritizing AI investment for HR; only 5% feel fully prepared. Growth/market-expansion rose 25 points as a CHRO priority since 2023.
- **Bersin (Superworker, 2025-01-14, fetched):** "For each dollar spent on machine learning technology, companies may need to spend nine dollars on intangible human capital." 30-40% of today's HR roles will be automated/transformed [snippet-verified, cite carefully].
- **Gartner (VERIFIED via RCR Wireless quoting primary, 2025-06-25):** 40%+ of agentic AI projects canceled by 2027; 15% of day-to-day decisions autonomous by 2028 (from 0%); 33% of enterprise software has agentic AI by 2028; only ~130 of thousands of "agent" vendors are real ("agent washing").
- **Gartner Guardian Agents (2025-06-11, snippet-verified - flag):** guardian agents = 10-15% of agentic market by 2030; 40% of CIOs will demand guardian agents by 2028.

## Frameworks (named)
- **Bersin Superworker** (verbatim): "an individual who uses AI to dramatically enhance their productivity, performance, and creativity." Four Stages of AI: Assistance -> Augmentation -> Replacement -> Autonomy.
- **Bersin Superagent (2026):** autonomous outcome-focused AI that moves "beyond streamlining tasks to replacing entire segments of the org chart." 2026 CHRO imperative #1: "Build unified AI architecture."
- **Gartner Guardian Agents** (governance layer overseeing other agents); "agent washing."
- **Korn Ferry: CHRO-as-architect** at "the intersection of business strategy, workforce planning, and talent development."

## Strong quotes (attributed, verified)
- Bersin: "the AI revolution... is all about redesigning the way we get things done. And that lands in the laps of HR: how we redesign, reskill, and redeploy people."
- Bersin: "now the HRBP manages the AI... someone behind the scenes is monitoring your trip." HR becomes overseers of "digital employees."
- Anushree Verma, Gartner: "To get real value from agentic AI, organizations must focus on enterprise productivity, not just individual task augmentation."
- Laura Manson-Smith, Korn Ferry: "Today's CHROs aren't just shaping the talent agenda. They're helping shape the entire strategic direction of the organization."

## Toil vs judgment (HR)
Agents take: scheduling, screening, L&D content, HR ops, first-draft comms, data prep. Stays human: care, judgment on people, culture, conflict, ethics, the decision about which work stays human.

## CRITICAL TENSION — do agents belong "on the org chart like employees"? (handle honestly)
The 2025-26 literature splits. Posts MUST engage this, not ignore it, or OTP looks naive vs recent HBR research.
- **Camp A (manage like coworkers):** MIT SMR "Agentic AI at Scale" (Sep 2025): "Agentic AI must be managed more like a human coworker than like a traditional tool" (69% of experts agree new management is required). HBR (Srinivasan & Wei, Feb 2026): a new human role, the **"agent manager,"** runs agents via dashboards/scorecards/observability. HBR (May 2025): agents are "digital teammates," an emerging talent category needing an HR/procurement playbook.
- **Camp B (do NOT treat like employees):** HBR/BCG (Kropp et al., May 2026, "Why You Shouldn't Treat AI Agents Like Employees"): anthropomorphizing agents in a large experiment "reduced individual accountability, increased unnecessary escalation, lowered review quality." Model: **"rented contractor with a narrow statement of work,"** governed by scoped permissions, kill switches, audit logs, and NAMED HUMAN OWNERS, not HR onboarding/titles/performance reviews. [verbatim quote via secondary Metaintro - flag; article verified on hbr.org but paywalled]
- **The synthesis OTP should land (and it is genuinely true to the product):** both camps agree on the substance: every agent needs a NAMED HUMAN OWNER, a MEASURED seat (observability/scorecard), and human-retained ACCOUNTABILITY. OTP's one-seat-one-owner chart delivers exactly that. It is not anthropomorphizing (Jeff the agent was retired by a human decision via a hearing; accountability never moved to the agent). The right framing is "accountability architecture," not "agents are people." MIT SMR (Vosloo): "agentic AI (i.e., software) cannot be accountable for its decisions" - the deploying human is.
- WRITING RULE: do not assert "onboard/performance-review your agent like an employee" flatly. Frame as: the agent gets a seat with a named owner and a scorecard; the human owner is accountable; "onboarding" = scoped permissions + a clear metric, "retiring" = a human decision when the seat is not earned.

## Verified stats from the second fork (Deloitte / SHRM / HBR / MIT SMR)
- **Deloitte 2025 Global Human Capital Trends** (~10,000 leaders, 93 countries, Mar 2025): 73% say middle-manager role reinvention matters but only 7% report great progress; managers spend ~40% of time on admin vs 13% on people development; only 6% of workers say their org is making great progress on AI value; 70%+ more likely to join an AI-prepared org. Frame = "boundaryless workforce" (NOT "superagency" - that's McKinsey).
- **SHRM State of AI in HR 2026** (1,908 HR pros): 62% use AI somewhere (39% in HR); adoption Recruiting 27% / HR-tech 21% / L&D 17%; governance gap: 49% have AI-use policies but only 25% call them clear; AI is "5.7x more likely to shift job responsibilities and 3x more likely to create new roles than displace jobs."
- **HBR Analytic Services agentic-AI survey** (603 leaders, Dec 2025, via Fortune): only 6% fully trust agents with core processes; 43% trust them with limited/routine tasks; 9% fully deployed, 50% piloting; 86% expect investment to rise; only 12% have risk/governance controls fully in place.
- **MIT SMR:** 69% of experts say agentic AI demands new management approaches. "Nine Essential Questions" Q9: "When agentic AI systems make critical decisions, how can companies ensure accountability?" Accenture (via MIT SMR): high-AI-value firms 4.5x more likely to have invested in agentic architectures.
